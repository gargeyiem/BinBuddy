Why Use Bin Buddy

People often confuse which bin to use, leading to 25% of recyclables being contaminated and wasted. Bin Buddy's Al ensures accurate sorting, reducing landfill waste and boosting recycling efficiency. This can cut greenhouse gas emissions by up to 70%, contributing to a greener planet.

Our efforts align with the UN's Sustainability Development Goal 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production), with the potential to help recycle up to 30% of the staggering 62 million tons of waste generated by India annually.

Type of Trash

PAPER

Paper is biodegradable and recyclable, commonly used in packaging, newspapers, and books.

Disposal Conditions:

Dry: Clean paper, free of food or grease, should go in the recyclable bin.

Wet: Wet or soiled paper (e.g., tissue) may need to go into compost or general waste, depending on local rules.

Examples:

  • Newspapers
  • Magazines
  • Office paper
  • Cardboard

Bins to use:

Recyclable bin (dry), Compost or general waste (soiled/wet).

Decompose in 2-6 weeks

GLASS

Glass is durable, 100% recyclable, and can be reused without loss of quality.

Disposal Conditions:

Dry: Rinse and remove lids before recycling.

Wet: Glass contaminated with food residue may not be accepted for recycling.

Examples:

Bottles, jars, window glass.

Bins to use:

Glass recycling bin (clean), General waste (contaminated).

Decompose in > 1 million years

METAL

Metal is recyclable and widely used in packaging, construction, and household items.

Disposal Conditions:

Dry: Rinse and flatten cans or tins for recycling.

Wet: Dirty or mixed-material metals may require special disposal.

Examples:

Aluminum cans, tin containers, steel scraps.

Bins to use:

Metal recycling bin (clean), Special waste disposal (mixed materials).

Decompose in 5 to 500 years

PLASTIC

Plastic is a synthetic material commonly used for packaging, household products, and industrial purposes.

Disposal Conditions:

Dry: Clean and dry plastics should be placed in recycling bins.

Wet: Contaminated or food-stained plastics may require washing before recycling or disposal.

Examples:

Water bottles, food containers, packaging films, straws.

Bins to use:

Plastic recycling bin (clean and dry), General waste bin (non-recyclable or soiled plastics).

Decompose in 5 to 500 years

Type of Bins

TRI-SORT SYSTEM

Wet Bin

What to put: Food scraps, organic waste, compostable items

What to avoid: Non-compostable items, E-waste or hazardous waste

Dry Bin

What to put: Recyclables like paper, cardboard, clean plastics

What to avoid: Food-soiled items, organic, hazardous or medical waste

Electronic Bin

What to put: Old electronics, small appliances, charges, and batteries

What to avoid: Everything that's not electronic

COMMON SORTING SYSTEM

Metal

What to put: Aluminium items, steel, tin, scrap metal, copper or brass.

What to avoid: Items with plastic or rubber, hazardous metals, e-waste.

Paper

What to put: Newspapers, magazines, books, office paper, cardboard.

What to avoid: Items with plastic or rubber parts, painted/rusted metals.

Plastic

What to put: Water bottles, food containers, plastic bags, packaging films.

What to avoid: Dirty or food-stained plastics, plastics like Styrofoam.

Glass

What to put: Glass bottles, jars, and other clean glass containers.

What to avoid: Broken glass, mirrors, ceramics, or light bulbs.

General Waste

What to put: Food scraps, non-recyclables, small non-hazardous items.

What to avoid: Recyclable items, electronic waste, hazardous materials.

COLOR CODED SYSTEM

Organic (Green)

What to put: Food scraps, vegetable waste, garden trimmings.

What to avoid: Plastic, metals, glass, or any non-biodegradable materials.

Recyclable (Blue)

What to put: Paper, cardboard, plastic, glass, aluminum cans.

What to avoid: Dirty recyclables, hazardous waste, or electronics.

Non Recyclable (Red)

What to put: Styrofoam, soiled tissues, non-recyclable plastics.

What to avoid: Recyclable materials, electronic waste, hazardous items.

Hazardous (Yellow)

What to put: Batteries, medical waste, chemicals, fluorescent light bulbs.

What to avoid: Organic or recyclable materials, general waste.
